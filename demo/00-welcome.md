# Welcome to MD Reader

You're reading this inside the app right now. That is, in a sense,
the point: **MD Reader turns any folder of `.md` files into a book**.

There's nothing to install in the folder. No build step, no
config. Drop a directory, get a sidebar, get a reader. The files
stay plain Markdown on disk; the app is a smart viewer for them.

---

# What this tour covers

This folder is a tiny book. Each chapter teaches one aspect of
the app, usually by demonstrating it. Read in order, or jump to
the one you need.

- **01-reading**. Typography, font size, content width, dark mode.
- **02-navigation**. Sidebar, library, recents, search.
- **03-editing**. The inline WYSIWYG editor, rename, bookmark.
- **04-html**. `html` fences rendered as real DOM.
- **05-svg**. `svg` fences rendered as the graphic itself.
- **06-slides**. Deck view for chapters with `---` breaks.
- **07-mermaid**. Diagrams inside markdown.
- **08-excalidraw**. Interactive scenes you can edit in place.
- **09-pdf**. Exporting a chapter to PDF.

If you only have two minutes, read the next section and skim
the rest.

# How to navigate

Three ways to move around:

- **Sidebar**. Every `.md` file is a chapter; subfolders become
  parts. Click any title on the left to jump to it.
- **Sibling arrows**. At the bottom of every chapter there are
  *Previous* and *Next* links that follow the sidebar order.
- **Keyboard**. `←` and `→` move between chapters. `Esc` exits
  anything modal. `Ctrl/Cmd + F` searches within a chapter.
  The search box in the toolbar does the same thing.

The progress bar at the top of the chapter remembers where you
stopped. The app also remembers your last scroll position per
file, your bookmarks, your font size, your light/dark mode. All
local, all in `~/Library/Application Support/com.mdreader.app/`.
